Michael McClurkin, MD, MPP

As a Resident Psychiatrist with Yale School of Medicine's Department of Psychiatry, I specialize in the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of mental, behavioral, addictive, and emotional disorders such as schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders, mood disorders, anxiety disorders, substance-related disorders, sexual and gender identity disorders, and adjustment disorders. My previous research covered a wide range of topics, including community-based interventions to optimize the use of web-based and wearable technology to improve cardiovascular health, and psychosocial epidemiology.

I graduated with a BA in Biological Sciences (2013) from State University of New York at Buffalo, a Master of Public Policy (2019) from Harvard Kennedy School, and a Doctor of Medicine (2019) from Harvard Medical School. My graduate education was funded with generous support from Harvard Kennedy School's Zuckerman Fellows Program through the Center for Public Leadership and the Rappaport Institute for Greater Boston through the Public Policy Summer Fellowship. Before graduate school, I worked as a researcher with Dr. Tiffany Powell-Wiley of the National Heart Lung and Blood Institute & National Institute on Minority Health and Health Disparities.
