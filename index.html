Michael McClurkin, MD, MPP

As a Resident Psychiatrist with Yale School of Medicine's Department of Psychiatry, I specialize in the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of mental, behavioral, addictive, and emotional disorders such as psychotic disorders, mood disorders, anxiety disorders, substance-related disorders, sexual and gender identity disorders, and adjustment disorders. My clinical work is across three major departmental clinical facilities: Connecticut Mental Health Center (CMHC), Veterans Administration Connecticut Healthcare System (VACHS), Yale-New Haven Hospital (YNHH), and two affiliated facilities, Yale Health and the Yale Child Study Center (CSC).

I graduated with a BA in Biological Sciences (2013) from State University of New York at Buffalo, a Master of Public Policy (2019) from Harvard’s John F. Kennedy School of Government, and a Doctor of Medicine (2019) from Harvard Medical School. My graduate education was funded with generous support from Harvard Kennedy School's Zuckerman Fellows Program through the Center for Public Leadership and the Rappaport Institute for Greater Boston through the Public Policy Summer Fellowship. Before graduate school, I worked as a researcher with Dr. Tiffany Powell-Wiley of the National Heart Lung and Blood Institute & National Institute on Minority Health and Health Disparities.
